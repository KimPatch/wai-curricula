## Introduction

Resources on how to plan, manage and apply accessibility within organizations. Overview on organizational planning and managing strategies to ensure accessibility is part of the process, and on different roles and responsibilities which have an influence on accessibility implementations. Emphasis on how involving people with disabilities in the whole design and development process has a positive impact on all users. Policies on how to implement accessibility and adopt measures that promote accessibility interim repairs in an on-going project.

**TIP** &mdash; The business context that learners are used to may also require adjustment of the suggested considerations and strategies. For example, policies often apply differently to public and private bodies.

{% include excol.html type="all" %}

## Learning outcomes

* Understanding different organizational strategies to plan and manage accessibility
* Awareness on how to measure the accessibility capability and maturity of organizations
* Getting acquainted with different roles and responsibilities that have an influence in accessibility.
* Understanding the importance of involving people with disabilities throughout
* Knowledge on how to identify current accessibility issues in an on-going project.
* Knowledge on how to prioritize issues and seek for interim repairs.

## Prerequisites

***Learners***
:   [Basic] Basic understanding of computers and the Web.

***Instructors***
:   [Medium] Good understanding of how to introduce and integrate accessibility strategies throughout organizational processes.

## Suggested teaching outline

This is a suggested teaching outline to help achieve the learning outcomes. Instructors might choose to adapt the sequence of the topics or their content according to the particular audience and context.

{% include excol.html type="start" %}

### Topic: Planning and managing web accessibility

provides resources on how to apply and manage accessibility within organizations or companies.

{% include excol.html type="middle" %}

#### Learning outcomes

* Understanding that accessibility implementation needs commitment from all team members.
* Understanding that resources need to be put in place at a technical and at a financial level so that accessibility can be integrated.
* Awareness on the role that accessibility plays within Quality Management processes.
* Awareness of real examples of consistent approaches to accessibility from well-known companies and organizations.

{% comment %}

#### Approximate duration.

20-25 minutes.

{% endcomment %}

#### Suggested teaching activities 

* **Presentational:** Introduce specific considerations to [integrate accessibility throughout the design process](#planning-and-managing). i.e.:
	* Ensuring management commitment to accessibility
	* Assigning appropriate roles and responsibilities
	* Providing technical and financial resources
	* Ensuring accessibility is integrated into Quality Management processes.
	* Explain how the capability and maturity of an organization with regard to web accessibility can be assessed based on the above considerations.
* **Presentational**: Provide resources about [involving real users with disabilities](#involving-users) early on in the design process, and throughout the development and maintenance stages of websites.
* **Presentational**: Provide [real examples of organizations or companies](#business-case) that have developed a consistent set of accessibility strategies.
* **Conversational**: &mdash; Ask students to provide and comment on [real examples of organizations or companies](#business-case) that have developed a consistent set of accessibility strategies.

#### Suggested homework

Ask students to write a short essay on how they would integrate accessibility strategies in an organization. Ask them to enumerate various roles in charge of accessibility among the team and what would be their main responsibilities.

{% include excol.html type="end" %}

### Topic: Accessibility roles and responsibilities

Provides information on  several roles and responsibilities in charge of the accessibility-related tasks.

{% include excol.html type="middle" %}

#### Learning outcomes

* Understanding how accessibility responsibilities are shared among different roles and positions within the team.
* Knowledge on some accessibility-related tasks and their mapping to specific roles and functions.
* Appreciation for the need of organizational approaches towards accessibility implementations.

{% comment %}

#### Approximate duration.

20-25 minutes.

{% endcomment %}

#### Suggested teaching activities

* **Presentational:**
	* Explain the different types of [roles](#role_definition_document) involved in implementing web accessibility.
	* Introduce the scope of their responsibilities throughout the design and development process.
	* Explain that everyone involved in the process needs to have an adequate level of accessibility awareness and skills.
	* Emphasize that accessibility is not only a technical discipline but it also needs to be taken into account as an organizational aspect.
* **Conversational**: Promote discussion with students around a particular accessibility feature and try to help them identify the specific role within the team who would be in charge of it. Reflect on whether this would be the only role involved or this feature might require help from other team members.

#### Suggested homework 

Ask students to write a short essay on the main accessibility roles and responsibilities they have been introduced to. Encourage them to put especial attention to the ones they may be more involved in if they work in accessibility.

{% include excol.html type="end" %}

### Topic: Developing policies on web accessibility

Provides resources on how to develop accessibility policies and statements for organizations or companies.

{% include excol.html type="middle" %}

#### Learning outcomes

* Understanding the importance of accessibility policies to ensure and reinforce commitment among the organization members.
* Awareness on some current accessibility approaches from well-known organizations.

{% comment %}

#### Approximate duration.

30 minutes.

{% endcomment %}

#### Suggested teaching activities 

* **Presentational:** Provide information about putting [accessibility policies](#org-policies) in place:
	* to document organizational commitments
	* to encourage implementation of web accessibility.
	* to define monitor and review processes.
* **Conversational**: Ask students to reference publicly available [accessibility statements](#statements) and encourage them to reflect on some of the aspects they include.

#### Suggested homework 

Ask students to [draft an accessibility statement](#statements) taking into account a particular product of their choice.

{% include excol.html type="end" %}

### Topic: Accessibility in an on-going project

Provides resources to apply accessibility characteristics in an on/going project, such as detecting issues and pursuing interim repairs.

{% include excol.html type="middle" %}

#### Learning outcomes

* Understanding the current status of the project with regards  to accessibility.
* Knowledge of some of the strategies to prioritize issues and pursue interim repairs.

#### Suggested teaching activities

* **Presentational:**
	* Introduce some of the methods to gain an understanding of the [current accessibility status](#interim-repairs) in the project. i.e.: performing a first review of Web Accessibility or Carrying out a detailed accessibility evaluation.
	* Introduce some of the strategies that can be used to prioritize issues and pursue interim repairs, which can become necessary when accessibility is not considered from the beginning of the design and development process.
* **Conversational**: Engage students in a conversation based on a set of accessibility issues. encourage them to reflect on which of the issues they would prioritize based on what they have already learned. Encourage them to refer not only to the issues themselves but to elaborate on how they would solve them.

#### Suggested homework
Ask students to write a short essay based on a particular digital product to identify which they would consider to be the most relevant issues present. Encourage them to establish the priorities and elaborate on how the issues could be solved.

{% include excol.html type="end" %}

## Ideas for Assessment

* **Knowledge Questions** &mdash; Learners should be able to enumerate different roles and responsibilities involved in web accessibility, and different organizational considerations to plan for and manage implementation.
* **Reflections** &mdash; Learners should be able to reflect on the capacity and maturity of their own organizations regarding web accessibility with respect to (lack of) accessibility processes and practices put in place.

## Suggested Teaching Materials

* <a id="planning-and-managing" href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/planning-and-managing/">Planning and Managing Web Accessibility</a> &mdash; Enumerates and explains a broad selection of organizational considerations relating to web accessibility. These are organized according to web project management stages of "Initiate", "Plan", "Implement", and "Sustain", to help fit into organizational processes.
* <a id="involving-users" href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/planning/involving-users/">Involving Users in Web Projects for Better, Easier Accessibility</a> &mdash; Explains the benefits of involving real users with disabilities from the inception of web projects throughout their design and development stages, and provides advice on working with people with disabilities effectively.
* <a id="business-case" href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/business-case/">The Business Case for Digital Accessibility</a> &mdash; Explains the rationale for organizations to pursue digital accessibility for people with disabilities. This includes driving innovation, enhancing brand, extending market reach, and minimizing legal risk. It includes cases studies from different organizations.
* <a id="role_definition_document" href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/EO/wiki/Role_definition_document">(Work in progress) Accessibility Roles and Responsibilities Mapping (ARRM): Role Definition</a> &mdash; Defines a variety of roles relevant for accessibility throughout the design and development process. It includes examples tasks in accessibility for each of the roles defined.
* <a id="org-policies" href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/planning/org-policies/">Developing Organizational Policies on Web Accessibility</a> &mdash; Provides step-by-step guidance on developing organizational policies relating to web accessibility. It is part of the broader "Planning and Managing Web Accessibility" resource, to help guide through this particular organizational step.
* <a id="statements" href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/planning/statements/">Developing an Accessibility Statement</a> &mdash; Provides guidance and a free generator tool to help website and application owners to create accessibility statements. It encourages organizations to communicate their commitment to accessibility and to provide feedback channels for user with disabilities.
* <a id="interim-repairs" href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/planning/interim-repairs/">Web Accessibility First Aid: Approaches for Interim Repairs</a> &mdash; Introduces approaches to help organizations prioritize and address pressing issues, while building towards more comprehensive approaches to implement accessibility. It is part of the broader "Planning and Managing Web Accessibility" resource.
* <a id="contact-inaccessible-websites" href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/teach-advocate/contact-inaccessible-websites/">Contacting Organizations about Inaccessible Websites</a> &mdash; Provides guidance for users with disabilities on how to contact organizations when they observe accessibility barriers. It encourages communication and exchange between organizations and their website users.
